Did you know?

You can make the biggest impact on your bill with simple behavioural changes.

Up to a third of all household water use goes down the toilet, most of it perfectly good drinking water.

The average person in Ireland uses 150 litres of water a day. By 2020 the demand for water could increase by 800 million extra litres of water a day.

Most of this water is used for washing and toilet flushing, but it also includes drinking, cooking, car washing and watering the garden. We use almost 50 per cent more water than 25 years ago, partly because of power showers and household appliances.

Using water in our homes contributes around 35 million tonnes of greenhouse gases a year. The average family uses 500 litres of water a day (due to some communal use of appliances). That’s equal to 1.5 tonnes of greenhouse gases a year.

97.5% of the planets water is saltwater...and this, unfortunately, is unfit for human consumption.

More than 90% of the planet’s fresh water supply is in Antarctica.The vast bulk of the earth's freshwater out of reach as it is locked in polar snow and ice. Less than one percent of freshwater actually counts as usable. This usable amount is actually 0.01% of the Earth’s water. This means that every living thing on earth has to share an extremely limited supply of Earth’s available freshwater.

Each day, humans must replace 2.4 litres of water. This is done through drinking liquids and food consumption. We absorb the water from the foodstuffs we are eating.

Human brains are 75% water...Our bones are 25%, and human blood is 82% water. A chicken is 75% water, a tomato 95% and an elephant 70%!

The past century has seen a tenfold increase in consumption. There are 1.1 billion people living today who do not have any access to clean drinking water. Also 2.4 billion people are lacking proper sanitary provision.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is water conservation?

Water conservation is the use of practices and technologies that reduce levels of water consumption. Conservation frees water for other uses, like population growth and environmental needs.

Why is water conservation important?

Only 1% of the earth's water is avaiable for human consumption. Demand for fresh clean water is increasing. Reducing water consumption and waste is an important component of maintaining this scarce resource.

Why is fresh water important?

70% of fresh water is used for agriculture. Without fresh water we would starve. A person could survive for three weeks without food, but could only last for three days without water.

What are the main issues?

About 1 billion people are facing water scarcity. Population growth is pushing demand beyond supply. 2.6 billion people do not have safe dring water.

In my home, what activity uses most water?

Flushing the toilet is the largest single use of water in the home. Toilets use about 10 litres of water for each flush. This is about 40% of average household usage.

How can I help to conserve water?

Calculate your water usage with the calculator. Read the water conservation tips. Follow the helful links and find out more about water conservation.
